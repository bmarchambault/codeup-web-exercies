National Park Facts You Didn't Know

First Federally Protected Land of the U.S

It wasn't Yellowstone. President Andrew signed The Hot Springs into legislation in 1832 to have it as a protected area.

Who First Took Care of the Parks

Before the the establishment of the National Park Service, the U.S. Army Cavalry was responsible for caretaking between 1886 - 1916 .

U.S. Doesn't Own all the National Parks.

The national park of American Samoa is the only U.S. protectorate land that is leased from local villages. A new lease is negotiated every 50 years.

No One Used to Care if Bears ate Human Food

The National Park Service used to have viewing areas were people could watch bears riffle through dumpsters. At one point, Glacier National Park threw deceased horses over a certain cliff for the grizzlies waiting below - all for the sake of human spectating.

How Theodore Roosevelt National Park got its Name

It was were Roosevelt went to mourn the death of his wife and mother (who died on the same day). He felt the desolate landscape of the badlands in North Dakota reflected his heart.
